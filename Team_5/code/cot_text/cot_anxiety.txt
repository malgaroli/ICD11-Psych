#### Step 2: Symptom Evaluation
Reflect on these questions to evaluate the symptoms in detail:
    1. Is there evidence of persistent, excessive worry that isn’t linked to any specific situation (free-floating anxiety)?
    2. Is this worry broad, affecting various areas of life (e.g., work, health, family)?
    3. Are there other associated symptoms such as muscle tension, sleep problems, or irritability?

#### Step 3: Duration and Impact
Examine how long the symptoms have persisted and their effect on functioning:
    1. Have the symptoms been ongoing for several months?
    2. Do they significantly affect the individual’s personal, social, or work life?

#### Step 4: Final Diagnosis
Provide the most accurate diagnosis for the patient or conclude no current diagnosis if none align sufficiently. Include a summary of your reasoning and highlight any critical symptoms or observations that informed your decision.
